# Tracking a Copy's History

Every [copy](../3-core-concepts/5-items-and-copies.md) you own can carry its full story: what you paid, what it is worth, who insured it, who borrowed it, the care it has received, where it came from, and where it has lived. Each part of that story is its own dated record, and this section walks through creating each one.

All of these pages share the same starting point. Open an item, go to its **History** tab, and pick the copy you want to work on. They also share one prerequisite: if you have not read [A copy's history explained](../3-core-concepts/11-copy-history.md), start there, because it explains why the records work the way they do.

Adding or changing records requires the editor or owner role. Viewers can read everything but change nothing.

Read the pages in any order, or follow the story of a copy from purchase onwards:

1. [Record what you paid and what it is worth](2-record-payments-and-value.md). Transactions for money, valuations for worth.
2. [Insure a copy](3-insure-a-copy.md). Keep policy details next to the thing they cover.
3. [Lend and borrow copies](4-lend-and-borrow-copies.md). Track custody without losing track of ownership.
4. [Record maintenance and repairs](5-record-maintenance-and-repairs.md). Log cleaning, servicing, and restoration.
5. [Trace a copy's provenance](6-trace-provenance.md). Build its ownership and authenticity narrative.
6. [Move a copy and keep its location history](7-move-a-copy.md). Change where it lives without forgetting where it was.
7. [Attach documents to a copy](8-attach-documents.md). Receipts, certificates, and appraisals, stored with the copy.
8. [Read the copy timeline](9-read-the-copy-timeline.md). See every record merged into one chronological story.

If you only track one thing, track transactions and valuations. They power the value figures across KolleK, including your [collection statistics](../7-insights/2-collection-statistics.md).
