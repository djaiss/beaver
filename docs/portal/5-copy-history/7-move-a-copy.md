# Move a copy and keep its location history

When you picked a [location](../3-core-concepts/9-locations.md) while creating a copy, you told KolleK where it lives. Moving is different: it changes where the copy lives *while remembering where it used to be*. Over the years, that becomes a trail of everywhere the copy has been kept.

## How location history works

A copy's location history is a chain of records. At any moment, exactly one record is open, and that open record is the copy's current location. Recording a move closes the previous record and opens a new one, stamped with the date and the reason.

So "where is it" and "where has it been" are the same data, read from different ends.

## Record a move

Priya moves her 1968 Omega Speedmaster from her desk drawer to the safe after its new valuation.

::::steps
:::step title="Open the copy's history"
Open the item, go to its **History** tab, select the copy, and open the **Locations** section.

::screenshot{label="History tab with the Locations section open"}
:::

:::step title="Choose where it is going"
Record a move and pick the **new location** from your account's locations. If the right spot does not exist yet, an owner or editor can add it first (see [Set up your locations](../6-organizing/7-set-up-your-locations.md)).
:::

:::step title="Say when and why"
Set the **date** of the move, a **reason** ("Higher valuation, moved to the safe"), and any **note**. Save it.
:::
::::

The copy now shows the safe as its current location, and the drawer has become history with a start date, an end date, and a reason for leaving.

## Fixing a mistake

Correcting a past record is possible. If you mistyped a date or picked the wrong shelf, edit the record rather than adding a fake move to compensate. The history should reflect what actually happened.

:::note
Moving is for real, physical relocations. If you are reorganizing your storage itself, renaming a shelf or nesting a box somewhere else, change the location in [Set up your locations](../6-organizing/7-set-up-your-locations.md) instead. Copies keep pointing at it, and no move records are needed.
:::

## Why bother with the history

For everyday copies, the current location is all you will ever check. The history earns its place with valuable ones: an insurer may ask where a piece has been kept, a buyer may care that it spent a decade in a climate controlled cabinet, and you may simply want to know where something was the year it got scratched. Because moves are dated, the location history lines up against loans, maintenance, and condition changes in the [timeline](9-read-the-copy-timeline.md).
