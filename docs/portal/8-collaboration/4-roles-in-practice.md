# The three roles in practice

This page is a quick reference of what each role can actually do. For the ideas behind the model, read [accounts, users, and roles](../3-core-concepts/3-accounts-users-and-roles.md) first.

The short version: every member can read, editors and owners can write, and only owners can administer the account.

## Capability table

| What you want to do | Viewer | Editor | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Browse collections, items, and copies | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| See statistics, activity, and the dashboard | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Create and edit collections, items, and copies | No | Yes | Yes |
| Record copy history (transactions, valuations, loans, and the rest) | No | Yes | Yes |
| Upload photos and attach documents | No | Yes | Yes |
| Set up collection types, custom fields, tags, and locations | No | Yes | Yes |
| Use the trash (restore or permanently delete) | No | Yes | Yes |
| See the member list and pending invitations | No | No | Yes |
| Invite, change roles, and remove members | No | No | Yes |
| Change account settings (name, default currency, checklist) | No | No | Yes |
| Delete the account | No | No | Yes |

## How to read this

**Viewers are safe pairs of eyes.** Leo, invited as a viewer, can browse Emma's comics, open any item, read a copy's history, and admire the statistics. He cannot add, change, or delete anything. Viewers suit family members who want to look things up, or club members who should see the shared catalogue without editing rights.

**Editors are collectors at work.** Sam, invited as an editor, can do everything involved in cataloguing: create collections, add items and copies, record history, tag, file, and organize. Editors suit anyone actively maintaining the catalogue.

**Owners are also administrators.** On top of everything an editor can do, owners manage the people and the account itself. Every account needs at least one, and KolleK will not let the last owner be demoted or removed.

:::note
These roles apply to your whole account, not to individual collections. A collection's own visibility setting records who it is meant for, and once sharing is enforced it will decide who can see it, while roles keep deciding who can change it. See [visibility and sharing](../3-core-concepts/12-visibility-and-sharing.md).
:::
