# Core Concepts

KolleK has a specific data model, and it is worth twenty minutes of your time. Once you understand the handful of ideas in this section, every task page in the rest of the documentation becomes obvious. Read these pages once, early, and come back whenever a term is fuzzy.

Read the pages in this order:

1. @doc(kollek.howOrganized). The whole mental model on one page.
2. @doc(accounts.usersAndRoles). The workspace, the people in it, and what each person may do.
3. @doc(collections.overview). The named groups that hold everything you catalogue.
4. @doc(items.itemsVsCopies). The single most important idea in KolleK.
5. @doc(collectionTypes.overview). How you decide what each kind of item records.
6. @doc(organizing.categoriesSetsAndSeries). Three grouping tools, each answering a different question.
7. @doc(tags.overview). The lightest way to label and slice across items.
8. @doc(locations.overview). How physical storage is modelled.
9. @doc(conditions.overview). How the state of a copy is graded.
10. @doc(copyHistory.concept). Where value, movement, and provenance live.
11. @doc(sharing.overview). Who can see a collection, and when.
12. @doc(dataSafety.howProtected). Encryption, the audit trail, and their limits.

If you only have five minutes, read @doc(items.itemsVsCopies). It is the one page the rest of the documentation leans on constantly.
