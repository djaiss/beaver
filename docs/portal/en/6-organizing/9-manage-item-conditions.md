# Manage item conditions

A @doc(conditions.overview, "condition") grades the physical state of a copy. This page covers managing the condition list itself from account settings, as opposed to grading a single copy, which is covered in @doc(copies.track).

You need the editor or owner role.

## Start from the defaults

A fresh account comes with five starter conditions: New, Like New, Used, Worn, and Damaged. They are not locked in any way: rename them, delete them, or add to them like any condition you create yourself.

## Create, rename, and delete

Open **Item conditions** in account settings to see every condition in the account.

- **Create** a condition ahead of time when your hobby needs a grade the starter list does not cover, such as a specific term from a formal grading scale.
- **Rename** a condition to fix a typo or change its wording. Renaming updates the label everywhere the condition is already applied.
- **Delete** a condition you no longer want, including one from the starter list.

:::warning
Deleting a condition removes it from every copy currently graded with it. Those copies are not deleted, they simply go back to having no condition recorded, and there is no undo for the removal.
:::

## Keep the list meaningful

Conditions are most useful when everyone who catalogues agrees on what each grade means.

- **Agree on wording early** if several people catalogue together. Decide what "Used" means for your hobby once, and apply it consistently. See @doc(conditions.overview, "grading consistently").
- **Keep the list short.** A handful of grades you actually use beats a long list that nobody remembers the difference between.
- **Use a custom field for a formal scale.** If your community grades on a fine-grained scale (coin collectors and their MS numbers, for example), a Select @doc(collectionTypes.overview, "custom field") on the type can hold the precise grade while the condition keeps your practical, everyday one.
