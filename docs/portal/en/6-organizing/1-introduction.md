# Organizing Your Catalogue

Adding items is only half the work. This section covers the structure around them: the types and custom fields that decide what each item records, the categories, sets, and series that group them, the locations that map your physical storage, the tags that cut across everything, the conditions that grade your copies, and the photo library that keeps your images tidy. Most of these are set up once, then refined as your collection grows.

Read the pages in whatever order matches what you are setting up:

1. @doc(collectionTypes.setup). Define what each kind of item records.
2. @doc(collectionTypes.importExport). Move a type definition in or out as JSON.
3. @doc(categories.organizeItems). Build a nested filing tree inside a collection.
4. @doc(sets.trackCompletion). Know what you own versus what you still need.
5. @doc(series.groupFranchise). Tie items together across collections.
6. @doc(locations.setup). Map where things physically live.
7. @doc(tags.manageAccount). Curate the shared tag vocabulary.
8. @doc(conditions.manage). Curate the list of grades your copies can carry.
9. @doc(photos.library). Work with every image in one place.

If the difference between categories, sets, and series is fuzzy, start with the concept page on @doc(organizing.categoriesSetsAndSeries, "categories, sets, and series") before the how to guides.
