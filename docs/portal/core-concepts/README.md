# Core Concepts

KolleK has a specific data model, and it is worth twenty minutes of your time. Once you understand the handful of ideas in this section, every task page in the rest of the documentation becomes obvious. Read these pages once, early, and come back whenever a term is fuzzy.

Read the pages in this order:

1. [How KolleK is organized](how-kollek-is-organized.md). The whole mental model on one page.
2. [Accounts, users, and roles](accounts-users-and-roles.md). The workspace, the people in it, and what each person may do.
3. [Collections](collections.md). The named groups that hold everything you catalogue.
4. [Items versus copies](items-and-copies.md). The single most important idea in KolleK.
5. [Collection types and custom fields](collection-types-and-custom-fields.md). How you decide what each kind of item records.
6. [Categories, sets, and series](categories-sets-and-series.md). Three grouping tools, each answering a different question.
7. [Tags](tags.md). The lightest way to label and slice across items.
8. [Locations](locations.md). How physical storage is modelled.
9. [Conditions](conditions.md). How the state of a copy is graded.
10. [A copy's history](copy-history.md). Where value, movement, and provenance live.
11. [Visibility and sharing](visibility-and-sharing.md). Who can see a collection, and when.
12. [How your data is protected](how-your-data-is-protected.md). Encryption, the audit trail, and their limits.

If you only have five minutes, read [Items versus copies](items-and-copies.md). It is the one page the rest of the documentation leans on constantly.
