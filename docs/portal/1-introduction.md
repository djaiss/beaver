# KolleK documentation

Welcome. KolleK is a private, self hostable home for everything you collect: comics, vinyl, coins, watches, wine, books, cards, video games, or anything else you can own. You create collections, describe them with your own custom fields, add items with photos and tags, and track every physical copy you hold, including its condition, storage location, value, and full history.

This portal is organized so a new reader can start at the top and never meet an advanced idea before they need it.

## The sections

1. @doc(gettingStarted.index). What KolleK is, creating your account, and your first catalogued item.
2. @doc(coreConcepts.index). The mental model: accounts, collections, items, copies, and everything around them.
3. @doc(coreFeatures.index). Day to day cataloguing: collections, items, copies, photos, and tags.
4. @doc(copyHistory.index). Money, value, insurance, loans, care, provenance, moves, and documents.
5. @doc(organizing.index). Types and custom fields, categories, sets, series, locations, tags, and the photo library.
6. @doc(insights.index). The statistics screen and the dashboard.
7. @doc(collaboration.index). Inviting people, roles, and sharing collections.
8. @doc(accountAndProfile.index). Personal details, language, avatar, and account settings.
9. @doc(security.index, "Security"). Two factor authentication, recovery codes, magic links, passwords, and API keys.
10. @doc(dataSafety.index). The trash, deletions, and getting your data out.
11. @doc(tutorials.index). Complete, end to end journeys that stitch the features together.
12. @doc(developers.index). Authentication, conventions, and webhooks for people building against KolleK.
13. @doc(selfHosting.index, "Self Hosting and Administration"). Installing, configuring, upgrading, backing up, and administering an instance.
14. @doc(troubleshooting.index). Quick answers when something does not behave as expected.
15. @doc(reference.index). The glossary, every field and status option, and every email KolleK sends.

## Where should you start

**If you collect things.** Read @doc(kollek.whatIs), @doc(accounts.create, "create your account"), follow @doc(gettingStarted.checklist, "the getting started checklist"), then learn @doc(kollek.howOrganized, "how KolleK is organized") and the key idea of @doc(items.itemsVsCopies, "items versus copies"). From there, the @doc(coreFeatures.index) guides carry you through daily cataloguing, and @doc(copyHistory.index) is waiting when you want to record value, loans, and provenance.

**If you own an account others use.** Everything a collector reads, plus @doc(accounts.usersAndRoles, "accounts, users, and roles"), the @doc(collaboration.index) section, @doc(accounts.settings, "account settings"), and @doc(dataSafety.index, "Data Safety").

**If you run an instance.** Start with @doc(kollek.hostingOptions), then work through @doc(selfHosting.index, "Self Hosting and Administration") from the @doc(selfHosting.index, "overview") onward, paying particular attention to @doc(selfHosting.applicationKeyAndEncryption, "the application key").

**If you are building against the API.** Read the @doc(api.overview) and @doc(api.authenticate, "authenticate with the API"), then use the generated endpoint reference at `/docs/api`. Read @doc(webhooks.overview, "webhooks") knowing that no event fires them yet.
