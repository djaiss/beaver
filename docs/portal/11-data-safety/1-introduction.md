# Data Safety and Housekeeping

Deleting things, recovering them, and knowing what is permanent should never be guesswork. This section makes the reversible and the irreversible unmistakable: what the trash catches and for how long, how to remove your own user, what deleting an account really destroys, and what data portability exists today.

Read the pages as you need them:

1. [Restore something from the trash](2-restore-from-trash.md). What the trash catches, how long it keeps things, and how to bring them back.
2. [Delete your user](3-delete-your-user.md). Remove yourself, as opposed to the whole account.
3. [Automatically delete an inactive user](4-inactive-user-deletion.md). The opt in self cleanup setting.
4. [Delete an account](5-delete-an-account.md). The most destructive action in the product.
5. [Back up your collection data](6-back-up-your-collection-data.md). What can be exported today, honestly.

The one rule worth carrying into every page here: if something is about to be permanent, KolleK will say so before you confirm. When in doubt, check the trash first, because most everyday deletions land there.
